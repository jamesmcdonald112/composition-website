# Market Research & Pricing Strategy — Solicitor Website Product

Compiled from market research, competitor analysis, and pricing benchmarks across Ireland and the UK.

---

## What We Know About the Market

### Ireland — the gap

There are approximately 2,000+ solicitor practices in Ireland. The vast majority have mediocre websites. The current supplier landscape breaks down into:

1. **Generic web agencies** — no sector knowledge, no legal compliance awareness, charge €2,500–€7,500 one-off, sell on price
2. **"Specialist" solicitor agencies** — WordPress template shops who have positioned themselves as legal specialists without doing the deep compliance work (Solicitorsite.ie, Nua, Web Wizard)
3. **Nobody** — building at a premium design level with legal compliance built in on a subscription model

The subscription/rent model is essentially non-existent in the Irish legal sector at quality. The one Irish provider who appeared to be doing it (Solicitorsite.ie) had a dead/403 website at time of research.

### UK — the benchmark market

The UK is 4–5 years ahead of Ireland on this. The most instructive comparison is **Conscious Solutions** — the UK's largest law firm digital agency with 300+ law firm clients. Their pricing publicly validates what the market will pay for a properly built, compliance-aware legal website product.

## Legal Compliance Monitoring — Market Gap

Research found **no standalone legal website compliance monitoring service** with published pricing in Ireland or the UK.

- **LSRA (lsra.ie):** Actively monitors solicitor websites for S.I. 644/2020 compliance. Does not offer prior approval or vetting. Compliance is entirely self-managed by the solicitor.
  - **Source:** https://www.lsra.ie/for-law-professionals/advertising-legal-services/

- **DG Legal (dglegal.co.uk):** Offers SRA compliance retainer services (UK) — bespoke pricing based on firm size. Includes compliance support, document templates, 25+ consultants. No published price.
  - **Source:** https://dglegal.co.uk/services/retainer-service/

- **43Legal (43legal.com):** SRA Compliance Manager services on retainer or fixed-fee. No published pricing.

**Conclusion:** No Irish or UK provider is offering "LSRA advertising compliance built into your website" as a named product feature. This is a genuine market gap and the single strongest differentiator available.

## Why Other Providers Don't Offer Compliance

This is worth understanding because it explains why the gap exists and how durable it is:

1. **Sector generalists** — most Irish web agencies work across every industry and don't go deep enough on any one sector to know the regulations
2. **Liability aversion** — agencies fear that claiming "compliance" implies they're giving legal advice. The correct framing — *"compliance-aware implementation"* not *"legal advice"* — sidesteps this entirely, but most agencies don't think through it
3. **No recurring revenue incentive** — one-off agencies build and move on. There's no business reason to invest in compliance knowledge if you're not selling a recurring product
4. **The regulation is recent** — S.I. 644/2020 came into force December 2020. Most agencies haven't caught up and likely never will unless pushed
5. **Reading and documenting the regulation is unglamorous work** — most developers avoid it; it's not technically hard but requires sustained attention

The result: this is a durable advantage. Competitors won't close this gap quickly because doing so requires changing their business model, not just adding a feature.

## Pricing Decision Framework

### The three variables to decide first

**1. Do you sell subscriptions, purchases, or both?**

| Model | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| Subscription only | Predictable recurring revenue, you retain leverage (site comes down if they stop paying), lower barrier to entry for client | Some clients will resist not owning their site, especially solicitors who are contractually minded |
| Purchase only | Clean transaction, no ongoing relationship needed | Large upfront number slows sales, no recurring income, you lose leverage after delivery |
| Both (recommended) | Client chooses, you benefit either way, buy-out option rewards long-term subscription clients | Slightly more complex to explain and contract |

**Recommended: Offer both.** Lead with subscription as the default. Have the purchase price high enough that most clients choose subscription. Offer a declining buy-out for subscription clients as a retention and upsell tool.

---

**2. Where do you position on quality?**

Three positions exist in the market:

- **Template/low-cost** (€75–€150/month, Ctrl Alt Elite, Solicitorsite.ie): Fast to build, low margin per client, needs volume. Race to the bottom.
- **Mid-market** (€150–€250/month): The majority of the market. Differentiated on compliance and service, not design.
- **Premium** (€250–€400/month): Custom design at Awwwards level, GSAP animation, full LSRA compliance system, premium photography direction. Needs fewer clients to hit meaningful revenue. This is where this product sits.

**Recommended: Premium positioning.** The codebase already justifies it. Trying to compete on price with template providers destroys the margin and the brand.

---

**3. How do you scale?**

The risk with a bespoke model is time. Every new client is a full build. Solutions:

- **Componentise the codebase** — this has already been done. Each new client reuses the same component library. Marginal build time falls with every client.
- **Standardise the content system** — config-driven content (already done) means client onboarding is filling in a config file, not rebuilding from scratch
- **Limit bespoke work** — define what's included in the base product and charge separately for anything outside it
- **Set a client cap** until systems are in place to handle more

Realistic build time per client once the template is mature: **5–8 days** for a full new site. At 2 builds/month that's €400–€560/month in new recurring revenue added per month.

## How to Strengthen the Position

### 1. Make the compliance documentation public-facing
The work done in `config-review/` is a strong selling tool. A public "Why LSRA compliance matters" page on your portfolio site, written for solicitors, will rank for searches like "LSRA solicitor website compliance" and "S.I. 644/2020 advertising" — terms no competitor is targeting.

### 2. Get case studies fast
Mary's site is client #1. As soon as it's live, get a quote from her about the process and the compliance work. A case study from an actual Irish solicitor carries enormous weight with other solicitors.

### 3. Partner with a contract solicitor
Have a solicitor review and draft your client contract. Two reasons: it'll be a better contract, and you can say "our client agreement was drafted by a solicitor" — which matters when your clients are solicitors.

### 4. Build a simple portfolio site
Not a mega project — a single-page or small Astro site showing the work. Include the compliance positioning prominently. This is the first thing you show prospective clients.

### 5. Compliance-specific cold outreach — the highest-conversion tactic

Search for Irish solicitor websites that contain "no win no fee", success rate claims ("95% success rate"), or personal injury inducement language ("Have you been injured?"). These firms have a live, documentable LSRA compliance risk. The outreach approach is specific, relevant, and creates genuine urgency.

**How to find targets at scale:**
- Google: `site:.ie "no win no fee"` — returns Irish sites using the phrase
- Google: `site:.ie solicitor "success rate"` — returns success rate violations
- Google: `site:.ie solicitor "have you been injured"` — returns inducement language
- Build a spreadsheet of firm name, URL, specific violation, contact email
- The Law Society's online directory (lawsociety.ie/find-a-solicitor) lists all ~2,000+ practices with contact details

**Volume reality:**
- 20 targeted outreach emails/day × 5 days/week = 100/week = ~4,000/year is achievable
- At 2,000 contacts/year × 20% response rate = **400 interested conversations**
- Even at a 5% close rate from those conversations = **20 new clients from outreach alone**
- Mass email (no site-specific violation) still converts at 2–5% — at 2,000 sends, that's 40–100 responses

### 6. Phone-first outreach — the highest-quality lead sequence

Email alone is low-friction for the prospect to ignore. A phone call first changes the dynamic entirely: you get a live response, you can handle objections in real time, and a subsequent email lands as a follow-up rather than cold contact.

**The three-touch sequence:**

| Touch | Channel | When | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Phone call | Day 1 | Get a live conversation. Leave a voicemail if no answer. |
| 2 | Email | Day 1 (same day as call) | Reference the call. Include the compliance issue. Add the visual preview link. |
| 3 | Follow-up email | Day 5–7 | Soft bump. One line. No pitch. |
| 4 | Final email | Day 14 | Close or drop. "Leaving you alone after this one." |

**The phone script (compliance-led, 60 seconds):**

> *"Hi, is that [Name]? My name is James, I'm a web developer based in [location] and I specialise in solicitor websites. I was looking at your website today and I spotted something that might be worth flagging — your site has a phrase that's caught by Regulation 6(b) of the LSRA advertising regulations, the 'no win no fee' prohibition. These are actively monitored. I can send you over a quick note about it and show you what a compliant version of your site could look like. Would that be useful? What's the best email?"*

Key points:
- Open with who you are and why you're calling — don't bury it
- Name the specific regulation — shows you know what you're talking about
- Ask a yes/no question that's easy to say yes to ("would that be useful?")
- End with a micro-commitment: "what's the best email?"

**The follow-up email (compliance + visual preview):**

Subject: *[Firm name] — LSRA Regulation 6(b) / compliance issue on your current site*

> *Following on from my call today — I wanted to send you the detail as promised.*
>
> *Your current site uses the phrase "no win no fee" in your personal injury section. Under Regulation 6(b) of S.I. No. 644/2020 (Legal Services Regulation Act 2015 Advertising Regulations), this phrase is specifically prohibited in advertisements for personal injury services. The LSRA monitors solicitor websites and this is one of the most commonly investigated violations.*
>
> *I've put together a quick example of what a compliant version of your homepage could look like: [link to mockup]*
>
> *We build solicitor websites with LSRA compliance handled from the ground up — not retrofitted. If you'd like to see the full picture, I'm happy to set up a 20-minute call.*
>
> *James*

### 7. AI-assisted personalised mockups — Option B at full scale

Previously, building a firm-specific visual mockup for every prospect was only viable for high-value targets (30–45 minutes of design work per contact). With AI tools (Claude, Midjourney, or similar), a credible personalised mockup showing how the prospect's firm's site could look takes **under 5 minutes**.

This means Option B — the personalised visual preview — is now viable for every outreach contact, not just the top tier.

**What the mockup should show:**
- The prospect's actual firm name and logo (if findable)
- A homepage layout with their practice areas surfaced
- The LSRA cost warning correctly placed
- Visually premium — clearly a step above their current site

**How to produce it fast:**
1. Screenshot their current site
2. Open a new AI-assisted design session — use Claude or similar to adapt the Mary Molloy base template with their firm name, area, and colour palette
3. Export a single image or short video walkthrough
4. Host on a short URL (e.g. a Netlify preview deploy or a simple Notion page)
5. Include the link in the follow-up email

The bar is not perfection — it's "clearly better than what they have now." Even a rough preview creates a specific, visual reason to reply.

**The volume implication:**
- At 20 contacts/day, 5 mockups takes 25 minutes total
- The personalised mockup group converts at a meaningfully higher rate — if even 50% of contacts get a mockup and that doubles the close rate from 5% to 10%, you get 40 clients from 2,000 contacts instead of 20
- At €240/month average that's the difference between €115,200 and €57,600 in annual recurring revenue at scale

### 8. One content piece that generates inbound indefinitely
A single well-written article — *"What Irish solicitors need to know about LSRA advertising compliance in 2025"* — would rank with minimal competition. No Irish web agency is targeting these search terms. Searches like "LSRA website compliance solicitor", "S.I. 644/2020 advertising solicitor", "Irish solicitor advertising regulations website" have essentially no competition in organic results.

This piece:
- Ranks passively and generates inbound leads without ongoing effort
- Positions you as the expert before the prospect has even contacted you
- Can be submitted to the Law Society Gazette or similar for additional reach
- Should link to your portfolio site prominently

### 9. Annual billing incentive — locks clients in and improves cash flow
Offer 2 months free on annual billing:
- Monthly: €199/month = **€2,388/year**
- Annual: €199 × 10 = **€1,990/year** (client saves €398)

Benefits:
- Client commits for 12 months upfront — eliminates churn risk for that period
- You receive a lump sum (or guaranteed annual revenue) — better cash flow predictability
- At 50% of clients on annual billing, your revenue visibility improves dramatically
- The saving is meaningful to a solicitor (nearly €400) but the annual amount (under €2,000) still feels modest

### 10. Consider the Law Society angle
The Law Society has a practice support team that fielded questions about compliance before the LSRA took over the advertising regulation role. There may be an opportunity to be listed as a recommended resource or supplier — worth exploring.
