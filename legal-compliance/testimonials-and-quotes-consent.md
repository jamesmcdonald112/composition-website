# Testimonials and pull-quote consent

The Mary Molloy Solicitor website may publish two kinds of named statements that fall under Reg 5(a)(i) of S.I. No. 644/2020 — the Legal Services Regulation Act 2015 (Advertising) Regulations 2020:

1. **Client testimonials** — statements made by a client of the firm, attributed to that client by name (or in a way that would identify them).
2. **Solicitor pull quotes** — statements made by a named solicitor in the firm (Mary, Nicholas, or Richard), used as a quote on a service or other page.

Both categories require **prior written consent** from the named person before publication. The form the consent takes is different in each case — see the two sections below.

The firm retains the record of consent. The website manager does not hold it.

---

## What Reg 5(a)(i) requires

The regulation requires *prior written consent* of any person whose name, photograph, or quotation appears in advertising material. Verbal consent does not satisfy the rule. A consent obtained after publication does not satisfy the rule (it must be *prior*).

The local source is `legal-compliance/Legal Services Regulation Act 2015 (Advertising) Regulations 2020.md`. The skill that interprets this rule for the website is `.claude/skills/irish-legal-compliance/SKILL.md`.

The four points any sufficient consent must cover, regardless of form, are:

1. **Awareness of publication.** The signer is aware their testimonial or quote will be published on the firm's website.
2. **Consent to the specific wording.** The exact text being published is reproduced and the signer confirms they consent to that wording. (If the wording later changes, a fresh consent is required.)
3. **Right to withdraw.** The signer is told they can withdraw consent in writing at any time. The firm will remove the testimonial or quote within a reasonable period of receiving a withdrawal request.
4. **Date and signature (or written equivalent).** The consent is signed and dated, or — for an internal email — the email is dated and shows the named person as the sender confirming the wording.

---

## Category 1 — Solicitor pull quotes (internal to the firm)

A pull quote attributed to a named solicitor in the firm (e.g. *"We always give clients an honest assessment from the start" — Richard O'Shea, Solicitor*) requires Reg 5(a)(i) consent because the named person's quotation is appearing in advertising material. But the relationship is internal: the firm is publishing the quote of one of its own solicitors on its own website. A formal client-style consent letter is disproportionate.

### What is sufficient

A short written confirmation from the named solicitor — by email, signed memo, or any internal written form — that:

- They have seen the exact wording proposed for publication
- They confirm they are happy for that wording to be published on the specific page named
- They understand they can withdraw consent in writing at any time and the firm will remove the quote within a reasonable period

A simple email such as:

> *"Yes — happy with the wording 'We always give clients an honest assessment from the start…' on the personal injury page. Richard, 30/04/2026."*

is sufficient and satisfies Reg 5(a)(i).

### What the firm retains

The email (or other written form) is the firm's record. It is retained in firm records under Reg 11(f) for at least 12 months from the latest date of publication.

### What changes if the wording or page changes

If the wording is edited, or the quote moves to a different page on the site, a fresh confirmation is required. A regulator's question on audit would be *"is there written consent for the wording currently published?"* — the answer must be yes.

---

## Category 2 — Client testimonials (external)

A statement attributed to a client of the firm, such as a Google review republished on the website or a quote from a thank-you note, is treated more carefully. The relationship between the firm and the client is professional, and obtaining a formal record of consent protects the firm and the client equally.

### What is sufficient

A short, dated, signed letter on the firm's letterhead — drafted in any plain wording — covering the four points from the regulation. The client signs and dates it; the firm retains the original.

A consent obtained verbally, or implied from the existence of a Google review, does not satisfy Reg 5(a)(i).

### What the firm retains

The signed letter is the firm's record. It is filed in the firm's client records under Reg 11(f), retained for at least 12 months from the latest date of publication.

### Special case — third-party platform reviews

A Google review (or a review on any third-party platform) is not automatic consent for republication on the firm's website. The reviewer signed up to publish their review on Google, not on Mary Molloy Solicitor's website. Republishing on the firm's website requires a separate signed consent from the reviewer.

If the consent cannot be obtained, the review remains on the third-party platform only and is not republished on the firm's website.

## Procedure between the firm and the website manager

When the firm wants to add a testimonial or pull quote to the website:

1. **The website manager flags that consent is required** before any named statement goes live and shares this document with the firm so the firm knows what is needed.
2. **The firm obtains the consent** in the appropriate form (Category 1 or Category 2 above) and retains the record.
3. **The firm confirms to the website manager** — by email, text, or in person — that consent has been obtained for the specific wording being published.
4. **The website manager records the date and method of confirmation** in their own working log (e.g. *"Mary confirmed Richard's consent for the personal injury pull quote, 30 April 2026, by email."*) and publishes the named statement.
5. **If the wording later changes**, the website manager flags it and the cycle starts again — fresh consent is required, the firm confirms, the website manager records and publishes.

The website manager's working log is internal. It records the *fact and date* of the firm's confirmation, not the consent record itself.

## Standard reference in Mary-facing review documents

When a content-review document at `legal-compliance/content-review/[NN]-[page].md` covers a page that has a testimonial or pull quote, the **Action items** section should reference this file rather than repeat the rule.

Standard wording for a **solicitor pull quote**:

> **Action:** the pull quote attributed to [name] on this page requires prior written consent under Reg 5(a)(i). For a quote by a solicitor in the firm, a short written confirmation by email — covering the four points set out in `legal-compliance/testimonials-and-quotes-consent.md` — is sufficient, and is retained by the firm. Please let the website manager know once that confirmation is on file.

Standard wording for a **client testimonial**:

> **Action:** the testimonial attributed to [name] on this page requires prior written consent under Reg 5(a)(i). A short signed letter from the client — covering the four points set out in `legal-compliance/testimonials-and-quotes-consent.md` — is sufficient, and is retained in firm records. Please let the website manager know once the signed consent is on file.

If a page has no testimonial or pull quote, no action item is needed.
