# Testimonials and pull-quote consent

Reg 5(a)(i) of S.I. No. 644/2020 (the LSRA Advertising Regulations 2020) requires **prior written consent** of any person whose name, photograph, or quotation appears on a solicitor's website. This file sets out how to satisfy that rule for each category of named content.

The firm retains every signed record. The website manager records the date the firm confirms a record is on file, then publishes — they do not hold the records themselves.

---

## What every consent must cover

A signed, dated record (letter, form, or email signed by the named person) covering:

1. Awareness that the named content will be published on the firm's website.
2. Consent to the specific content — wording, photo, role label — being published.
3. Right to withdraw in writing at any time, with the firm removing the content within a reasonable period.
4. Date and signature.

If the content later changes, fresh consent is required.

---

## The four categories

| Category | Applies to | Consent required |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Owner-solicitor** | Names, photos, biographical content, and quotes attributed to the firm's owner(s) | None — the firm publishing on its own behalf is the consent. Editorial sign-off on the page review is the audit record. |
| **2. Non-owner solicitor in the firm** | Same content type, but for any solicitor who does not own the firm (associates, employed solicitors, non-owning partners) | A single signed consent form per solicitor, on the firm's letterhead, covering their name, role, photo, biography, and any pull quotes (each quote and the page it appears on listed). Fresh consent if any covered element changes. |
| **3. Client testimonial** | Statements attributed to a client by name, including republished Google reviews | Signed consent letter from the client. Verbal consent or the existence of a third-party-platform review is not enough. |
| **4. Quoted third party** | Statements attributed to a solicitor at another firm, expert, witness, journalist, etc. | Signed letter or dated email from the named person. **If the third party is another solicitor, Reg 11(c) also applies** — both consents can be captured in one document. |

---

## Why the non-owner solicitor category uses a signed form

Either a signed form is on file or it is not — there is no interpretive question about whether an email or page sign-off counted as consent. That removes risk from both the firm and the website manager.

---

## Standard Action item wording in Mary-facing review documents

If the page contains content from **Category 1**, no Action item is needed. For the other three:

- **Category 2:** "the page names [solicitor name] and includes their photograph, role label, biographical content, and/or pull quote. A signed consent form for [solicitor name] must be on file with the firm — see `legal-compliance/testimonials-and-quotes-consent.md`. Please confirm to the website manager once the form is signed."
- **Category 3:** "the testimonial attributed to [client name] requires a signed consent letter from the client — see `legal-compliance/testimonials-and-quotes-consent.md`. The firm retains the letter. Please confirm to the website manager once the letter is signed."
- **Category 4:** "the quote attributed to [name] requires written consent — signed letter or dated email — see `legal-compliance/testimonials-and-quotes-consent.md`. (If [name] is a solicitor at another firm, Reg 11(c) also applies.) Please confirm to the website manager once consent is on file."
