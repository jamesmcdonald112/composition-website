### Aside

- **Title:** Property handled with care

**Intro:**

> On a residential conveyance, the firm acts on the title investigation, the contractual stage, the lender requirements where there is a mortgage, completion, and the post-completion steps with the Property Registration Authority and Revenue.

**Key points:**

1. **Contracts reviewed in full** — We read the contract in full and raise pre-contract and requisition enquiries with the seller's solicitor where needed before contracts are signed.
2. **Title investigated against the PRA** — We investigate the title at the Property Registration Authority — confirming the seller's ownership, identifying any judgment mortgages or registered burdens, and checking that the boundaries match the ground.
3. **Lender and stamp-duty work handled** — We act for the lender on a mortgage transaction, manage the drawdown of funds in time for completion, and calculate and file the stamp duty return with the Revenue Commissioners.
4. **Costs discussed at the outset** — We discuss our fees with each client at the outset, including the basis on which we charge and the third-party outlays (Land Registry fees, search fees, stamp duty) involved in the matter.

# Technical compliance summary

_For the website's records. You can skip this unless you want to read it._

This page was rewritten in full from the previous version. As the highest-traffic page on the site and the visitor's first impression of the firm, the home page concentrates almost every compliance category we cleared elsewhere in the audit. The rewrite removes six Reg 4(a)(iv) outcome promises, two Reg 4(a)(iv) superlatives, one Reg 4(a)(vi) question hook, one Reg 4(a)(ii)-bordering dramatising line, and one accuracy fix on the Law Society wording.

**Regulations reviewed:**

- **Reg 4(a)(i)** — likely to bring the profession into disrepute. The previous "you can rely on" / "a team that treats your matter as it deserves to be treated" framing was rewritten in calm, descriptive language.
- **Reg 4(a)(ii)** — likely to be in bad taste. The previous aside intro said "Buying or selling a home is one of the biggest decisions you will ever make" — the same dramatising-distress pattern we removed from the property service page. Replaced with a calm description of the firm's conveyancing work.
- **Reg 4(a)(iii)** — likely to constitute an attack on another solicitor. No comparative or critical wording. (The previous version was already clean on this point — the "not a call centre" attack we found on the services-index page is not on the home page.)
- **Reg 4(a)(iv)** — likely to be false or misleading. **The heaviest cleanup category on this page.** Six outcome promises removed and two superlatives removed:
  - *"You will always know where things stand"* (featured-service card body)
  - *"Keep you informed, and make the process as straightforward as possible"* (aside intro)
  - *"You are kept informed throughout"* / *"never left wondering"* (aside keypoint 2)
  - *"How to keep deals on track"* (aside keypoint 3 body — same phrase we removed from the property service page)
  - *"No surprises... exactly what to expect"* (aside keypoint 4 — outcome promise on cost)
  - *"A practice you can rely on"* (trust strip title — reliability superlative)
  - *"You can expect clear advice, consistent communication, and a team that treats your matter as it deserves to be treated"* (trust strip para 3)
  Plus two superlatives:
  - *"With over 46 years of experience"* (meta description)
  - *"Over 46 years of Kilkenny transactions"* (aside keypoint 3 heading)
  One accuracy fix: *"members of the Law Society of Ireland"* corrected to *"admitted to the Roll of Solicitors of Ireland and regulated by the Law Society of Ireland and the Legal Services Regulatory Authority"* — same fix as wills, property, leases, services-index, about pages.
- **Reg 4(a)(vi)** — likely to encourage a person to make a claim. The previous featured-service card title *"Buying or selling property in Kilkenny?"* was a direct question hook; rewritten to a descriptive heading. The previous primary CTA was the firm's phone number rendered as a "Call us on..." button — borderline 4(a)(vi) territory because a phone number as the primary action on the highest-traffic page is the textbook form of encouragement. The primary CTA has been changed to *"Our services"* (navigational); the phone number remains available in the header, footer, and contact page. The previous *"Call us today"* phrase has been removed from the meta description.
- **Reg 5(a)(i)** — consent to refer to a person. Mary Molloy is named as the firm's principal solicitor; as firm owner, separate consent is not required (Category 1 in `legal-compliance/testimonials-and-quotes-consent.md`). Nicholas O'Shea and Richard O'Shea are named as solicitors at the firm — covered by the firm-wide blanket consent forms they have signed. No content change required; no action item.
- **Reg 6(a)** — cost warning. Not directly applicable on the home page; the cost warning is rendered on the practice-area pages where contentious work is discussed and on the contact page.
- **Reg 6(b)** — no-win-no-fee ban. Not applicable; no fee-related wording.
- **Reg 6(c)** — referring to compensation amounts. Not applicable.
- **Reg 7** — claims of risk-free litigation. Not applicable.
- **Reg 8** — referring to specific success rates. No frequency claims appear.
- **Reg 11(f)** — record keeping. The firm retains dated screenshots of every page each time content changes, in `legal-compliance/compliance-records/`.

**Conveyancing-led structure preserved:** the home page leads with conveyancing (hero title, featured-service block, trust strip "Conveyancing is the largest area of the firm's practice"). This is a business-positioning choice and was preserved deliberately. The other seven practice areas are visible in the supporting-services block immediately below the conveyancing feature, and named in the hero subtitle and trust strip.

- Three Irish statutes anchored on the home page (Stamp Duties Consolidation Act 1999, Building Regulations, Property Registration Authority by reference)
- All eight practice areas anchored across the page (hero subtitle, supporting-services intro, trust strip para 2)

- *"Practised continuously on Rose Inn Street, Kilkenny since 1979"* — strong location and continuity SEO
