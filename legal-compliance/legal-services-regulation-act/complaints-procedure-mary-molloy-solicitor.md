## 1. Our commitment

We are committed to providing an efficient and professional service to all clients. If at any stage you are dissatisfied with the legal services provided by this firm, we encourage you to raise the matter with us as soon as possible so that we may attempt to resolve your concerns promptly and fairly.

---

## 2. What is a complaint

For the purposes of this procedure, a complaint is any expression of dissatisfaction about any service provided by this firm — whether about the legal work itself, the way we have communicated with you, our handling of your matter, or our fees.

---

## 4. How we will handle your complaint

Upon receipt of a formal complaint, we will acknowledge it promptly and will take all appropriate and reasonable steps to investigate and attempt to resolve the matter.

- We will acknowledge your complaint in writing within **7 days** of receiving it.
- We will then review the matter fully, including the relevant file.
- We will provide a full written response setting out our findings and any proposed resolution within **14 days** of acknowledgement.

If we need longer than 14 days to respond fully — for example because your complaint is complex or because the relevant solicitor is on leave — we will write to you within the 14-day window to explain the position and give you a revised timeframe.

---

## 5. Mediation

Where appropriate, and with the agreement of all parties, the matter may be referred to mediation as a means of reaching a fair resolution.

---

## 6. Possible outcomes

Depending on the circumstances, the outcome of a complaint may include one or more of the following:

- A full explanation of what happened and why
- A written apology
- A correction to our procedures so that the issue does not happen again
- A reduction or waiver of fees, where appropriate
- A referral of the matter to another solicitor at our cost, where appropriate

---

## 7. Complaints register

We keep a written record of every complaint received, the response given, and the outcome. The register is reviewed annually so that we can identify any patterns and improve the service we provide.

---

## 8. If you remain dissatisfied — escalation to the Legal Services Regulatory Authority

If you remain dissatisfied following the conclusion of our internal complaints process, you may refer the matter to the **Legal Services Regulatory Authority (LSRA)** in accordance with the provisions of the **Legal Services Regulation Act 2015**.

Under **section 51 of the Legal Services Regulation Act 2015**, the LSRA may investigate complaints that:

- The legal services provided were of an **inadequate standard**
- The costs sought were **excessive**
- An act or omission of the legal practitioner constituted **misconduct**

You do not have to go through this firm's procedure first — you can complain directly to the LSRA at any time.

**Time limits.** There is generally a three-year time limit for complaints about inadequate services and excessive costs. There is no time limit for complaints about misconduct.

## 9. Disputes about our fees specifically

If your concern is specifically about a bill of costs we have issued:

- Every bill we issue includes the statutory information required under **section 152(3) of the Legal Services Regulation Act 2015**, explaining your right to discuss the bill with us, to seek informal dispute resolution or mediation, and to apply for adjudication.
- You also have the right to apply to the **Office of the Legal Costs Adjudicator** for an independent assessment of the bill. Time limits apply: under **section 154 of the Legal Services Regulation Act 2015**, an application by a client cannot proceed after six months from when the bill was provided, or three months from when the bill was paid, whichever is earlier.
- You may also bring an excessive-costs complaint directly to the LSRA (see Section 8 above).

If you have any questions about how these routes apply to your bill, please contact Mary Molloy in the first instance.

## Sources — how this procedure was built and why each section is here

This complaints procedure has been drafted to satisfy the statutory and professional-conduct obligations that apply to an Irish solicitor's firm. Each section below cites the source it is based on.

### Section 1 — Our commitment

- Law Society of Ireland — Practice Note: Internal complaints procedure in solicitors' firms. Recommends opening with a statement that complaints are taken seriously. https://www.lawsociety.ie/Solicitors/knowledge-base/Practice-Notes/Internal-complaints-procedure-in-solicitors-firms/

### Section 2 — What is a complaint

- Law Society Practice Note (as above) — recommends defining what counts as a complaint.
- Industry-standard definition used by Irish firms (example: McKeever Rowan, https://www.mckr.ie/complaints-procedure/).

### Section 3 — How to raise a concern

- Law Society Practice Note — recommends a two-stage approach (informal first, then in writing).
- S.I. No. 533/2010, Reg 23 (EU Provision of Services Regulations 2010) — requires service providers to make complaint contact details available (postal address, email, telephone). Satisfied by providing all three. https://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/2010/si/533/made/en/print

### Section 4 — How we will handle your complaint

- Law Society Practice Note — recommends a written acknowledgement within 7 days and a full written response within 14 days.

### Section 5 — Mediation

- Legal Services Regulation Act 2015, section 61 — provides for informal resolution of complaints, including mediation. https://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/2015/act/65/section/61/enacted/en/html
- Mediation Act 2017 — establishes the framework for mediation in Ireland and the solicitor's duty to advise clients of mediation as an option. https://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/2017/act/27/enacted/en/html

### Section 6 — Possible outcomes

- Law Society Practice Note — recommends listing possible remedies including apology, procedural correction, fee abatement, and referral.

### Section 7 — Complaints register

- Law Society Practice Note — recommends maintaining a Complaints Register and reviewing it annually.

### Section 8 — Escalation to the LSRA

- Legal Services Regulation Act 2015, section 51 — sets out the three statutory grounds of complaint (inadequate services, excessive costs, misconduct). https://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/2015/act/65/section/51/enacted/en/html
- Legal Services Regulation Act 2015, section 53 — limitation periods. https://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/2015/act/65/section/53/enacted/en/html
- Law Society of Ireland — The complaints process — confirms the three-year limit on service/cost complaints and the absence of any limit on misconduct complaints. https://www.lawsociety.ie/complaints-handling/the-complaints-process/
- LSRA — Make a complaint — confirms LSRA postal address and that complaints must be in writing. https://www.lsra.ie/make-a-complaint/

### Section 9 — Disputes about fees specifically

- Legal Services Regulation Act 2015, section 150 — pre-engagement cost notice requirement. https://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/2015/act/65/section/150/enacted/en/html
- Legal Services Regulation Act 2015, section 152(3) — mandatory information on every bill of costs. https://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/2015/act/65/section/152/enacted/en/html
- Legal Services Regulation Act 2015, section 154 — adjudication time limits. https://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/2015/act/65/section/154/enacted/en/html

### General — that the firm has a procedure at all

- Law Society of Ireland — Guide to Professional Conduct of Solicitors (4th edition), Chapter 2 — recommends that every solicitor has a dispute-resolution procedure for dealing with complaints, communicated to the client. https://www.lawsociety.ie/globalassets/documents/committees/conduct-guide.pdf
- LSRA — Information Guide for Practitioners (July 2025) — confirms the role of informal resolution between firm and client before LSRA escalation. https://www.lsra.ie/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Information-Guide-for-Practitioners-July-2025.pdf

## How this procedure satisfies each obligation

Each obligation below is followed by the section of this document that satisfies it.

- **Have a written complaints procedure** (Law Society Guide to Professional Conduct, Chapter 2) — the document itself.
- **Communicate the procedure to the client** (Law Society Guide, Chapter 2) — provided as part of Terms of Business at engagement.
- **Define what counts as a complaint** (Law Society Practice Note) — Section 2.
- **Make complaint contact details available — postal, email, phone** (S.I. 533/2010, Reg 23) — Section 3 and document header.
- **Acknowledge in writing within a reasonable time** (Law Society Practice Note — 7 days) — Section 4.
- **Provide substantive written response within a reasonable time** (Law Society Practice Note — 14 days) — Section 4.
- **Offer mediation as a resolution route** (LSRA 2015 s.61; Mediation Act 2017) — Section 5.
- **Set out possible remedies** (Law Society Practice Note) — Section 6.
- **Keep a Complaints Register** (Law Society Practice Note) — Section 7.
- **Inform client of right to complain to the LSRA** (LSRA 2015, ss. 51 and 53) — Section 8.
- **Provide LSRA contact details** (LSRA published guidance) — Section 8.
- **Inform client of right to apply for cost adjudication** (LSRA 2015, ss. 152(3) and 154) — Section 9, and on every bill of costs.
- **Cover both service complaints and fee disputes** (LSRA 2015, Part 6 — same complaints regime applies to both) — Sections 8 and 9 combined.
