# Irish Solicitor Website Content Review Checklist
### S.I. No. 644/2020 — Legal Services Regulation Act 2015 (Advertising) Regulations 2020

**How to use this checklist:**
1. Run BASE COMPLIANCE checks on every page and config file
2. Identify the content type (personal injury, contentious, or general)
3. Run the relevant CONDITIONAL ADD-ON checks for that content type
4. Fix every flagged item before publishing

---

## PART 1: BASE COMPLIANCE
*Applies to every page, every config file, every piece of copy — no exceptions.*

---

### REG 4(a)(i) — CONTENT MUST NOT BRING THE PROFESSION INTO DISREPUTE

**What it means:** No content that a reasonable observer would consider damaging to the reputation of the legal profession as a whole. The test is not whether Mary finds it acceptable — it is whether the wider public or the LSRA would view it as undignified or harmful to trust in solicitors generally.

**Phrases to flag:**
No specific phrase list — apply a judgement test: would a senior Law Society member find this embarrassing or inappropriate?

**How to achieve compliance:**
Maintain professional tone throughout. Avoid casual slang, shock tactics, or anything sensationalist. When in doubt, write plainer and more formally.

**Examples of violations:**
- "Injured and fed up? Let's fight back."
- Aggressive or combative language framing litigation as a battle
- Anything that implies legal services are a product to be hustled

---

### REG 4(a)(ii) — CONTENT MUST NOT BE IN BAD TASTE

**What it means:** A separate prohibition from disrepute — this covers content that is tasteless, crude, or inappropriate even if it does not directly damage the profession's reputation. Humour, imagery, and tone all fall within scope.

**Phrases to flag:**
No specific phrase list — apply a taste test: would this copy appear in a quality national newspaper without causing offence?

**How to achieve compliance:**
Avoid dark humour, shock tactics, or graphic descriptions of injuries or distress. Content about serious matters (accidents, bereavement, family breakdown) must be handled with appropriate sensitivity.

**Examples of violations:**
- Humorous copy that makes light of road traffic accidents or workplace injuries
- Graphic descriptions of injury scenarios used as a hook to attract enquiries
- Flippant language on a wills or estate administration page

---

### REG 4(a)(iii) — NO ATTACKS ON OTHER SOLICITORS OR FIRMS

**What it means:** No content that reflects unfavourably on another legal practitioner — directly or by implication.

**Phrases to flag:**
- "unlike other firms..."
- "where other solicitors fail..."
- Any comparison that positions a named or unnamed competitor negatively

**How to achieve compliance:**
Focus entirely on Mary's strengths and services. Do not reference competitors at all.

**Examples of violations:**
- "Other firms charge hidden fees — we don't."
- "Where other solicitors give up, we keep fighting."

---

### REG 4(a)(iv) — NO FALSE OR MISLEADING STATEMENTS

**What it means:** Every factual claim on the site must be accurate and not create a false impression — even by omission or framing.

**Phrases to flag:**
- "expert in..." — unless Mary holds a formal specialist accreditation
- "leading solicitor", "top-rated", "best" — superlatives require objective evidence
- "guaranteed result" or any implied certainty about outcomes
- Any statistic or claim that cannot be verified

**How to achieve compliance:**
Use accurate, provable claims only. Experience, qualifications, years of practice, and service areas are all safe. Subjective superlatives without evidence are not.

**Examples of violations:**
- "Kilkenny's leading personal injury solicitor" — unprovable
- "We guarantee the best outcome for your case" — false certainty
- "Over 100 cases handled" — only if the actual number is lower

---

### REG 4(a)(v) — CONTENT MUST NOT BE PUBLISHED IN AN INAPPROPRIATE LOCATION

**What it means:** Advertisements cannot appear in hospitals, clinics, doctors' surgeries, funeral homes, cemeteries, crematoriums, or similar locations. For a website this applies to where the site is promoted or advertised — for example, placing online ads that target people in those physical locations, or distributing printed cards at a hospital.

**What to check on a website:**
This regulation applies primarily to physical placement of printed advertising and targeted digital ad campaigns — not to the website itself, which is accessed voluntarily. However, flag it if Mary ever runs paid digital ads (Google Ads, social ads) that specifically target people by proximity to hospitals, A&E departments, or clinics.

**Examples of violations:**
- Handing out business cards or leaflets in a hospital waiting room
- Running a Google Ads campaign geofenced to target people inside or near a hospital
- Placing a poster or advertisement on a notice board in a GP surgery

**Compliant approach:**
No advertising placement in any of the listed locations. Digital ads should not be geofenced to hospitals, clinics, or GP surgeries.

---

### REG 4(a)(vi) — NO ENCOURAGEMENT TO MAKE PERSONAL INJURY CLAIMS

**What it means:** You cannot use language that solicits, encourages, or induces people to make a personal injury claim. Listing personal injury as a service you offer is fine — the prohibition is on actively recruiting claimants.

**Phrases to flag:**
```
"Have you been injured?"
"You may be entitled to compensation"
"you may have a claim"
"find out if you can claim"
"accident wasn't your fault"
"been in an accident?"
"hurt at work?"
"don't suffer in silence"
"get the compensation you deserve"
"you could be owed money"
```

**How to achieve compliance:**
State that you handle personal injury matters. Do not frame the copy as a prompt for people to consider making a claim.

**Examples of violations:**
- "Have you been injured at work? You may be entitled to compensation — contact us today."
- "Road traffic accident? Find out if you have a claim."
- "Don't suffer in silence — you may have a right to compensation."

**Compliant alternative:**
- "We advise on personal injury matters, including road traffic accidents and workplace incidents."

---

### REG 5(a)(i) — PRIOR WRITTEN CONSENT REQUIRED FOR CLIENT REFERENCES

**What it means:** Before publishing any information about a specific client, case, or transaction, you must have prior written consent from that client. The client must have been shown the exact form, content, manner, and context of how their information will appear — not just a general description of it.

**Content to flag for consent check:**
- Named client quotes
- Any quote attributable to a specific person
- Case references that identify a client even without naming them ("a Kilkenny woman who...")
- Google Business reviews republished on the site
- Any mention of a specific case outcome

**How to achieve compliance:**
- Obtain signed written consent before publishing any testimonial
- Show the client the exact text and placement before going live
- Store a signed consent record for each testimonial — keep for 12 months minimum

**Examples of violations:**
- Publishing a Google review on the website without written permission from that reviewer
- "We helped John win his employment case" — even with name changed, if identifiable
- Quoting a client's relief at winning without their written consent

**Safe approach:**
Generic testimonials with no case details are lower risk but written consent is still recommended for all of them.

**Exemptions — no consent required for (Reg 5(b)):**
- Publicly available information (e.g. a court judgment already on public record)
- The bare fact that Mary acted in proceedings (e.g. "We acted in this case")

Both exemptions are void if using the information would compromise a duty of confidentiality.

---

### REG 5(a)(ii) — NO EXAGGERATION OF THE SOLICITOR'S ROLE

**What it means:** Even with consent, you cannot overstate or exaggerate the nature of your involvement in a case or transaction. The description of your role must accurately reflect what you actually did.

**Content to flag:**
- Any testimonial or case reference that implies sole credit for a complex outcome involving other parties or counsel
- Language that inflates a routine matter into an exceptional achievement
- "We won..." when the outcome was a settlement, or involved a barrister or other parties

**Examples of violations:**
- "Mary single-handedly secured our family home" — if other solicitors or counsel were involved
- Describing a standard conveyance as a "complex legal battle we overcame"
- Taking credit for a court outcome that was primarily secured by a barrister

**Compliant approach:**
Accurately describe what was done — "We advised on...", "We handled the legal aspects of...", "We represented the client in..."

---

### REG 8 — NO SUCCESS RATE CLAIMS — APPLIES TO EVERY PAGE

**What it means:** You cannot reference how often you win cases — expressly or by implication. This is an absolute ban. It does not matter if the statistic is true and verifiable — even a proven 90% win rate cannot be published.

**Phrases to flag:**
```
"success rate"
"win rate"
"% of cases"
"X% success"
"undefeated"
"consistently win"
"track record of success"
"successful outcomes"
"we win cases"
"proven track record"
"results-driven"
"outcome-focused"
```

Also flag any trust-strip statistics such as:
```
"X cases won"
"X successful claims"
"95% client satisfaction" (if tied to case outcomes)
```

**How to achieve compliance:**
Replace outcome language with experience language. Years of practice, types of matter handled, and specialist knowledge are all compliant. Outcomes are not.

**Examples of violations:**
- "We have a 94% success rate in personal injury cases." — prohibited even if true
- "Undefeated in employment tribunal claims."
- "Track record of successful outcomes for our clients."
- "We consistently win cases for our clients." *(implied success rate)*

**Compliant alternatives:**
- "We have extensive experience in personal injury litigation."
- "We handle complex employment law matters."
- "Specialist knowledge in road traffic accident claims."

---

### REG 9 — YOU ARE RESPONSIBLE FOR LINKED EXTERNAL CONTENT

**What it means:** If your website links to any external page that can reasonably be construed as promoting the firm, you are personally deemed responsible for that content — as if you published it yourself. This responsibility persists even if the linked page changes after you added the link. A third-party page containing prohibited content becomes your violation the moment you link to it.

**Links to audit:**
- Social media profiles (LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, X/Twitter)
- Google Business profile
- News articles mentioning the firm
- Legal directories (Solicitors.ie, any Law Society member listings)
- Partner or referral websites
- Any third-party page that references the firm by name

**How to audit each linked page:**
Check every linked page against all prohibited content rules — Reg 8 (success rates), Reg 6(b) (no win no fee), Reg 5 (client consent), Reg 4(a)(vi) (injury claim encouragement). If the external page contains prohibited content, remove the link or get the external page corrected before publishing.

**Safe links (generally compliant — informational only, no promotional content):**
- gov.ie, Courts Service, LSRA, Law Society of Ireland
- irishstatutebook.ie (legislation links)
- Purely informational legal resources with no promotional content about the firm

**Ongoing obligation:**
Audit all external links at launch and re-audit every 6 months. Linked pages can change — responsibility remains with the solicitor regardless.

**Google Business Profile — recommended approach:**
Do not link to the Google Business profile from the site. You cannot control what reviewers write, so any link creates a permanent liability — one review mentioning "she won my case" or "free consultation" puts you in violation through no fault of your own. The profile still functions independently via Google Search without a site link. If Mary wants to showcase reviews, the compliant route is to approach individual reviewers, obtain written consent, and republish only those specific approved reviews on the site — with any case outcome language removed.

**Get Directions link — recommended approach:**
Do not link directly to the Google Business profile or a Google Maps listing that shows the business panel with star rating and reviews. Instead, link to a Google Maps address search for the physical address only — this displays directions to the building without showing the business profile. Any click through to the business profile from there is the user's own action, not a link you published.

| Link type | Risk level | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Direct link to Google Business profile | High — promotional, you own the content | Remove |
| Direct link to Google Maps business listing (shows reviews panel) | Medium — grey area, functional argument but reviews visible | Avoid |
| Link to Google Maps address search only | Low — purely navigational, user must choose to click through | Use this |
| Plain text address, no link | Zero risk | Safest option |

**Example of a violation:**
- Linking to a Google Business profile that contains a review mentioning winning a case or offering a free consultation

### REG 10 — EDUCATIONAL CONTENT EXEMPTION

**What it means:** Genuine educational content — articles, books, media interviews, blog posts — is not treated as an advertisement and is exempt from all advertising regulations. But the exemption can be lost if the content is primarily promotional, paid for, or repeatedly republished.

**Three types of exempt content and when the exemption fails:**

#### 10(a)(i) — Books
| | Example |
|---|---|
| **Exempt** | Mary writes a guide to Irish probate law, sold commercially — genuinely informative |
| **Not exempt** | Mary prints copies of "Your Guide to Personal Injury Claims" and distributes them free at her office to attract clients — free distribution primarily to promote yourself strips the exemption under Reg 10(b) |

#### 10(a)(ii) — Articles
| | Example |
|---|---|
| **Exempt** | Mary writes an unpaid article for a legal journal explaining changes to employment law |
| **Not exempt (paid)** | Mary pays a local newspaper to publish her article — any paid placement strips the exemption |
| **Not exempt (repeated)** | Mary republishes the same article across her website, a legal directory, and LinkedIn — repeated republication in substantially the same form can strip the exemption under Reg 10(c) |

#### 10(a)(iii) — Oral presentations and media interviews
| | Example |
|---|---|
| **Exempt** | Mary appears unpaid on local radio to explain new rental legislation |
| **Not exempt (paid)** | Mary sponsors a podcast segment and is interviewed within it — paid placement strips the exemption |
| **Not exempt (repeated)** | Mary gives the same talk at multiple venues repeatedly — Reg 10(c) applies |

**Three-question check for any educational content:**
1. Is it genuinely informational rather than promotional?
2. Is any part of it paid for or sponsored?
3. Has it been published repeatedly in substantially the same form?

If the answer to question 1 is no, or questions 2 or 3 is yes — the exemption may not apply and all advertising regulations must be followed.

---

### REG 11 — FIRM IDENTITY MUST BE CLEAR

**What it means:** Every page must make clear who is responsible for the advertisement. The solicitor or firm name must be identifiable on every page — the solicitor cannot publish anonymously.

**Check for:**
- Firm name visible on every page — header, footer, or both
- No page that could appear as a standalone advertisement without identifying who published it

---

### REG 11(b) — PERSONAL RESPONSIBILITY

**What it means:** Compliance cannot be delegated. The solicitor is personally liable for every piece of content on the site regardless of who wrote or built it. Blaming a developer, copywriter, or agency is not a defence.

---

### REG 11(c) — WRITTEN CONSENT REQUIRED TO MENTION ANOTHER SOLICITOR

**What it means:** If any page references another solicitor or firm by name, prior written consent from that solicitor is required. This applies to referral arrangements, collaborations, testimonials from other practitioners, and any co-marketing.

**Flag:**
- Any named reference to another solicitor or firm
- Referral partner pages or co-marketing content
- "In association with..." or similar collaboration language

---

### REG 11(e) — PARTNERSHIP LIABILITY

**What it means:** In a firm with multiple solicitors — a partnership, multi-disciplinary practice, or limited liability partnership — every partner is personally liable for the firm's advertising compliance. It is not sufficient for one partner to approve content; all partners are deemed responsible.

**Applies to:** Any firm with more than one legal practitioner. Not applicable to a sole practitioner setup.

**If this applies:** All partners must be aware of and agree to the site content. A compliance review should be signed off by all partners, not just one. A violation by the firm is a violation by each partner individually.

---

### REG 11(f) — RECORD KEEPING (12 MONTHS MANDATORY)

**What it means:** Three categories of record must be kept for at least 12 months from the latest date of publication — and must be produced to the LSRA on request within that period:

| Record | When required |
|---|---|
| Copy of every advertisement | Always — website screenshots count |
| Written instructions given for publication | Where applicable — briefs to the developer, copywriter, or agency |
| Consent records | Client consents under Reg 5(a)(i) + solicitor consents under Reg 11(c) |

**The 12 months runs from the latest date of publication** — so if a page is still live, the clock hasn't stopped. Every live page needs a current screenshot on file.

**Recommended record-keeping system:**

Use a dated folder inside `legal-compliance/compliance-records/` for each milestone:

```
legal-compliance/
  compliance-records/
    YYYY-MM-launch/
      screenshots/        ← full-page screenshots of every live page
      instructions.md     ← paste all content instructions from Mary here, with dates
      consents/           ← PDFs of signed consent forms
    YYYY-MM-annual-review/
      screenshots/
      instructions.md
      consents/
```

- **Screenshots** — take at launch, then after any significant content change. GitHub is excellent for tracking what changed and when, but the LSRA needs to see the finished output as visitors see it, not source code. Keep both.
- **Instructions** — any content Mary sends by email, WhatsApp, or message to be published counts as a written instruction. Paste it into `instructions.md` with a date as you receive it. Takes 30 seconds and means you always have the record.
- **Consents** — PDFs of signed testimonial consent forms and any solicitor co-mention consents.
- **This checklist** — save a copy in each compliance folder as evidence the rules were known and applied.

**Before launch:**
- [ ] Create `legal-compliance/compliance-records/YYYY-MM-launch/` with screenshots, instructions.md, and consents/
- [ ] Screenshot every live page and save to `screenshots/`
- [ ] Copy this checklist into the launch folder
- [ ] Confirm all testimonial consent forms are saved in `consents/`
- [ ] Confirm written consent exists for any other solicitor mentioned by name

**After any content change:**
- [ ] Re-screenshot affected pages and save with a date stamp
- [ ] Paste any content instructions that triggered the change into `instructions.md`
- [ ] Re-audit external links if any new links were added

**Ongoing:**
- [ ] 6-month calendar reminder: re-audit all external links
- [ ] 12-month calendar reminder: full content review + new screenshot set in a new dated folder

#### REG 6(a) — COST WARNING IS MANDATORY

**What it means:** Any page that mentions personal injuries or contentious business must include a clear reference to the cost prohibitions in Section 149 of the Legal Services Regulation Act 2015. This is not optional.

**This requirement is triggered by any of these content types:**
- Personal injuries, road traffic accidents, workplace accidents, public place accidents
- Medical negligence, disease claims, any physical or mental harm claim
- Employment law disputes
- Debt collection
- Family court proceedings
- Any litigation, arbitration, or mediation
- Any other matter that could go before a court or tribunal

**Required warning text:**
> "In personal injury and litigation matters, if your case is unsuccessful you may be liable for the other party's legal costs. See Section 149 of the Legal Services Regulation Act 2015."

**Check:** Confirm the `<LegalCostWarning />` component is present in the site-wide footer — this covers all pages automatically. For any new service page covering the above content types, also confirm the warning appears on that page directly.

**Examples of violations:**
- A personal injury service page with no Section 149 reference anywhere on the page
- A footer redesign that removes the cost warning

---

#### REG 6(b) — "NO WIN NO FEE" AND SIMILAR PHRASES ARE BANNED

**What it means:** On any page that references personal injuries, you cannot use phrases that could be construed as meaning personal injury services will be provided at no cost to the client — directly or by implication. This applies to personal injury content only, not contentious business generally.

**Phrases to flag:**
```
"no win no fee"
"no foal no fee"
"free first consultation"
"free of charge"
"at no cost"
"without charge"
"costs nothing"
"nothing to pay"
"only pay if you win"
"pay nothing unless successful"
```

**How to achieve compliance:**
Do not reference cost arrangements on personal injury pages at all, or if you do, ensure the language accurately describes the actual cost agreement and includes the Section 149 warning.

**Examples of violations:**
- "We handle your personal injury case on a no win no fee basis."
- "Free first consultation for road traffic accident claims."
- "Only pay if your claim is successful."

**Compliant alternatives:**
- "Contact us to discuss your matter — we will advise you on all costs at the outset."
- "We will explain all potential costs at your initial consultation."

---

#### REG 6(c) — COMPENSATION AMOUNTS ONLY PERMITTED VIA PIAB CITATION

**What it means:** You cannot invent, estimate, or imply compensation figures. However, you *can* quote amounts directly from the PIAB Book of Quantum or other statutory guidelines — with attribution. The regulation permits this because official figures promote transparency and realistic expectations, not inflated promises.

**PIAB Book of Quantum:** https://www.injuries.ie/eng/forms-guides/book-of-quantum.pdf
**Local reference copy:** `legal-compliance/book-of-quantum.pdf`

**Flag — these are prohibited (invented or implied figures):**
- Any Euro amount (€) not attributed to the PIAB Book of Quantum
- "up to €X" without PIAB citation
- "average award of €X" without PIAB citation
- "typical payout", "you could receive €X", "claims worth €X"
- "Based on our experience, you could get €X"
- "We typically secure €X for similar cases"

**Permitted — PIAB-cited figures with attribution:**
- "According to the PIAB Book of Quantum, whiplash injuries range from €500 to €2,100"
- "The Book of Quantum shows fractured wrists typically receive €10,400–€21,000"
- Quoting any official PIAB range, clearly attributed to the Book of Quantum

**Examples of violations:**
- "Road traffic accident victims can receive up to €50,000 in compensation."
- "We typically secure €25,000–€50,000 for similar cases."
- "Clients usually receive around €40,000."

**Compliant alternatives:**
- "Compensation is assessed with reference to the Personal Injuries Assessment Board Book of Quantum."
- "According to the PIAB Book of Quantum, [injury type] ranges from €X to €X. Every case is assessed individually based on medical evidence."

#### REG 6(a) — COST WARNING IS MANDATORY (same as Add-On A)

The same cost warning requirement applies to all contentious business — the regulation covers "personal injuries or other contentious business." Confirm `<LegalCostWarning />` is present in the site-wide footer and inline on any new contentious service page.

**Required warning text:** Same as Add-On A above.

#### REG 7 — NO RISK-FREE LITIGATION CLAIMS

**What it means:** You cannot suggest — directly or by implication — that a client faces no risk of being ordered to pay the other side's legal costs in any contentious matter. Even implying this is prohibited.

**Narrow exception:** If there genuinely is no cost risk to the client in a specific situation (e.g. certain tribunal proceedings where costs orders are not legally available), you may say so — but only if that actually represents the legal position. This is rare.

**Phrases to flag:**
```
"worry-free"
"risk-free"
"no risk"
"nothing to lose"
"without risk"
"guaranteed"
"no financial exposure"
"fully protected"
"no hidden costs" (in litigation contexts — implies no cost risk)
"we absorb all the risk"
"you won't be out of pocket"
```

**Examples of violations:**
- "Pursue your employment claim worry-free with our expert team."
- "We take on your debt collection case with no risk to you."
- "Family law services with no financial exposure."
- "Nothing to lose — contact us today about your tribunal claim."

**Compliant alternatives:**
- "We will fully advise you on potential costs before you decide to proceed."
- "Employment disputes can involve cost risk — we will explain your position clearly from the outset."
- "We handle debt recovery matters and will advise you on any cost exposure at our first meeting."

## PART 3: PRE-PUBLISH QUICK REFERENCE

*Run through these questions for any new or changed content before it goes live.*

| Question | If YES → Action Required |
|---|---|
| Does it encourage people to make a personal injury claim? | **Remove** — prohibited under Reg 4(a)(vi) |
| Does it contain a false or misleading statement? | **Correct or remove** — prohibited under Reg 4(a)(iv) |
| Does it attack or reflect poorly on another solicitor? | **Remove** — prohibited under Reg 4(a)(iii) |
| Does it reference a specific client, case, or outcome? | **Get written consent first** — Reg 5 |
| Does it mention personal injuries, accidents, or contentious business? | **Confirm cost warning is present** — Reg 6(a) |
| Does it include "no win no fee" or similar phrases? | **Remove** — prohibited under Reg 6(b) |
| Does it mention a specific compensation amount? | **Remove unless citing PIAB Book of Quantum** — Reg 6(c) |
| Does it imply litigation carries no financial risk? | **Remove or rewrite** — prohibited under Reg 7 |
| Does it reference a success rate, win rate, or outcomes record? | **Remove and rewrite** — prohibited under Reg 8 |
| Does it link to an external page that promotes the firm? | **Audit that linked page** before publishing — Reg 9 |
| Has any existing page changed since the last screenshot audit? | **Re-screenshot and file** the new version — Reg 11(f) |

## QUICK REFERENCE: COMPLIANT VS PROHIBITED LANGUAGE

| Instead of... | Use... |
|---|---|
| "Track record of success" | "Extensive experience in..." |
| "We win cases for our clients" | "We handle complex [area] matters" |
| "No win no fee" | "Contact us to discuss costs at the outset" |
| "You could receive €X" | "Awards are assessed case by case" |
| "Have you been injured?" | "We advise on personal injury matters" |
| "Worry-free litigation" | "We will advise you fully on cost exposure" |
| "Leading solicitor in Kilkenny" | "Based in Kilkenny, serving clients across the region" |
| "Free first consultation" (PI) | "We will explain all costs at our initial consultation" |
| "Guaranteed result" | "We will advise you on the realistic prospects" |
