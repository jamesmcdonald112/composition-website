### PageRank and Internal Linking in 2025

**How Internal Links Work**

"Internal links are the 'plumbing' that moves PageRank from strong pages (home, linked assets) to priority URLs. Internal links pass PageRank just like external links do. When a high-authority page on your site links to a lower-authority page, some of that authority flows to the linked page."

*Source:* Rankability and DefiniteSEO
*URL:* https://www.rankability.com/ranking-factors/google/internal-links/

**Context and Relevance**

"Contextual links (in the main body of the article) are typically weighted more heavily than navigational, sidebar, or footer links because they are seen as editorial endorsements. Links with relevant, descriptive anchor text provide more contextual value than generic links like 'read more.'"

*Source:* Multiple sources including Rankability and DefiniteSEO
*URL:* https://definiteseo.com/on-page-seo/internal-linking/

**Role in Modern SEO (2025)**

"Internal links are not a single, standalone 'ranking factor' in the way external backlinks are—but internal links are a supercritical mechanism that shapes multiple direct and indirect signals (authority flow, discovery/indexation, topical relevance, UX). Every major ranking boost in 2024–2025 came from reorganising internal structure, not new links."

*Source:* Rankability
*URL:* https://www.rankability.com/ranking-factors/google/internal-links/

*URL:* https://theoceanmarketing.com/blog/what-should-the-title-tag-length-be-in-2025/

**Keyword Placement**

### Heading Hierarchy: H1, H2, H3 Rules

**H1 Guidelines**

"Each page should have one clear H1 that describes the main topic. Your H1 should be written naturally and should not be stuffed with keywords. It should read like a real headline written for humans."

*Source:* Yoast and Conductor Academy
*URL:* https://yoast.com/how-to-use-headings-on-your-site/

**H2 and H3 Structure**

"Use one clear H1 for the main topic, with H2s for main sections and H3s for sub-sections, maintaining logical hierarchy. For most pages, H2s do the heavy lifting. Use H3 tags when you need to break down a specific H2 section into smaller, related parts."

*Source:* Conductor Academy
*URL:* https://www.conductor.com/academy/headings/

**Critical Rule: No Skipping Levels**

"Do not skip heading levels. An H3 should always follow an H2, not jump directly from an H1. This keeps the structure logical for both users and machines."

*Source:* Hobo Web and SEO Sherpa
*URL:* https://www.hobo-web.co.uk/headings-seo-checklist/

**Descriptive vs Vague Headings**

"Your headings should describe what the section is about. Avoid vague labels such as 'Introduction' or 'More information.' Instead, write headings that clearly explain what the reader will learn in that section."

*Source:* Hobo Web
*URL:* https://www.hobo-web.co.uk/headings-seo-checklist/

**SEO Importance**

"Heading tags are vital for SEO because they provide semantic signals that help search engines understand a page's context and key topics. Google considers header elements a strong signal that informs the understanding of a page's topics, which can lead to better rankings and increased visibility."

*Source:* Conductor Academy
*URL:* https://www.conductor.com/academy/headings/

*URL:* https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/url-structure

**Avoid Keyword Stuffing**

**LocalBusiness vs LegalService**

"For solicitors and legal service providers, there's often confusion about whether to use LocalBusiness schema or LegalService schema, but the answer is both—LegalService is a more specific type that extends from LocalBusiness. *Source:* Schema.dev and OneFirst Legal

"Use LocalBusiness schema on your homepage and contact page to establish your physical location and NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency. ### Core Web Vitals: Thresholds and Impact

**The Three Metrics (Good Thresholds - 75th Percentile)**

"The three Core Web Vitals are Largest Contentful Paint (LCP), which measures loading speed with a 'good' threshold of under 2.5 seconds; Interaction to Next Paint (INP), which measures responsiveness with a 'good' threshold of under 200 milliseconds; and Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS), which measures visual stability with a 'good' threshold of under 0.1."

*Source:* Web.dev, RoastWeb, and CoreWebVitals.io
*URL:* https://web.dev/articles/defining-core-web-vitals-thresholds

**Complete Threshold Breakdown**

**LCP (Largest Contentful Paint):**
- Good: 2.5 seconds or less
- Needs Improvement: 2.6 to 4.0 seconds
- Poor: Over 4.0 seconds

**INP (Interaction to Next Paint):**
- Good: 200 milliseconds or less
- Needs Improvement: 201 to 500 milliseconds
- Poor: Over 500 milliseconds

**CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift):**
- Good: 0.1 or less
- Needs Improvement: 0.1 to 0.25
- Poor: Over 0.25

*Source:* CoreWebVitals.io and RoastWeb
*URL:* https://www.corewebvitals.io/core-web-vitals

**Important Note on Scoring**

"Google measures CWV at the 75th percentile of real user data (meaning 75% of page loads must meet thresholds to pass). All three must be passing at the 75th percentile for a page to achieve an overall 'good' Core Web Vitals assessment."

*Source:* Web.dev and Increv
*URL:* https://increv.co/academy/core-web-vitals/

**INP Replacement of FID**

"Since March 2024, Interaction to Next Paint (INP) has been an official part of Core Web Vitals, replacing First Input Delay, measuring website responsiveness to all user interactions."

*Source:* Multiple sources
*URL:* https://www.corewebvitals.io/core-web-vitals

**Why It Matters for Local Solicitor Sites**

Core Web Vitals are a ranking factor for all search results. Sites that don't meet these thresholds will struggle with visibility, and the mobile experience is particularly critical given that 60%+ of web traffic comes from mobile devices.

### Mobile-First Indexing

**What It Means**

"Google uses the mobile version of a site's content, crawled with the smartphone agent, for indexing and ranking. This is called mobile-first indexing. Mobile-first indexing means regardless of how high-performing your desktop is, its mobile counterpart will now hold greater sway in determining your search engine rankings."

*Source:* Google Search Central and SEO.com
*URL:* https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/mobile/mobile-sites-mobile-first-indexing

**Current Status (2025)**

"By July 5, 2024, Google fully transitioned remaining desktop-crawled sites to mobile crawling, making it the default for 100% of indexed websites. With smartphones and tablets generating over 60% of web visits, Google now looks primarily at the mobile version of your site when deciding where you'll appear in search results."

*Source:* Google Search Central and multiple sources
*URL:* https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/mobile/mobile-sites-mobile-first-indexing

**Content Parity Essential**

"Key actions include ensuring content parity between mobile and desktop versions, with identical meta tags, structured data, headings, and metadata."

*Source:* Google Search Central
*URL:* https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/mobile/mobile-sites-mobile-first-indexing

**Common Mobile Mistakes to Avoid**

- Avoid noindex tags on mobile pages
- Don't block resources with robots.txt that are needed for rendering
- Use responsive design or dynamic serving (not separate URLs)
- Test thoroughly on actual mobile devices

*Source:* Google Search Central
*URL:* https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/mobile/mobile-sites-mobile-first-indexing

**Impact of Non-Optimization**

"If your site is not optimized for mobile, you risk: Lower rankings in Google search results due to poor mobile usability and Core Web Vitals."

*Source:* SEO.com
*URL:* https://www.seo.com/blog/mobile-first-indexing/

### 301 Redirects

**What They Are**

"A 301 redirect is an HTTP status code that sends users and search systems from the requested URL to a different URL and indicates that the move is permanent."

*Source:* Semrush and multiple sources
*URL:* https://www.semrush.com/blog/301-redirects/

**SEO Impact**

"Using 301 redirects generally doesn't affect your SEO because they transfer search engine rankings and ranking signals from old pages to replacement pages, and search engines like Google typically transfer almost all of the old page's ranking power to the new page. Additionally, Google now confirms that properly implemented redirects can pass 100% of link equity."

*Source:* Semrush and Rankability
*URL:* https://www.semrush.com/blog/301-redirects/

**Best Practices for 2025**

1. **Use 301 for Permanent Changes** - If the change is permanent, always use a 301 status code.

2. **Redirect to Relevant Content** - "Always redirect to relevant pages, not generic ones like the homepage. For a redirect to keep its SEO value, it should deliberately point to a page with similar content as the original page."

3. **Maintain Redirects for Extended Period** - "Google recommends keeping permanent redirect configurations active for at least one year to ensure all search engines and external sites recognize the change, and for high-authority pages or those with many backlinks, consider keeping the 301 status code active indefinitely."

4. **Avoid Redirect Chains** - "Chains waste link equity and slow crawling. Use a crawler like Screaming Frog to identify them, then configure your url redirects to point old URLs directly to the final web page."

5. **Update Internal Links** - Update internal links and sitemaps to reflect the changes.

6. **Monitor with Tools** - Monitor redirects regularly using tools like Google Search Console to catch errors early.

*Source:* Semrush, Shawn Savvy, and Rankability
*URL:* https://www.semrush.com/blog/301-redirects/

### AREA 13: ANSWER ENGINE OPTIMIZATION (AEO) AND GENERATIVE ENGINE OPTIMIZATION (GEO)

These are distinct disciplines from traditional SEO. Understanding them is essential for 2025-2026 planning.

#### What Is AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)?

**Definition:** Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is the process of making your law firm's information easily found, understood, and recommended by AI-powered answer engines.

**Key Difference from Traditional SEO:** Traditional SEO focuses on ranking in search engines like Google. AEO focuses on ensuring AI assistants such as ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity can find, understand, and recommend your firm in their answers.

#### What Is GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)?

**Definition:** Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of structuring your content so AI platforms like ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, and Claude cite your firm when answering legal questions.

#### The AI Search Reality for Legal Queries

The scale of AI search in the legal sector is larger than most assume:

> "77.67% of legal search queries now trigger AI Overviews — more than any other industry vertical."

> "When they get an answer from ChatGPT or Google's AI Overview, 60% never click through to a website. They just trust what the AI tells them."

> "As of our latest study, over 28% of consumers now use ChatGPT to research lawyers."

> "Four out of five people now rely on AI-generated summaries for at least 40% of their searches."

**Market Impact:** LLM-referred traffic to legal websites more than doubled between early 2024 and mid-2025.

**Critical caveat from existing research:** While 77.67% of *legal queries* trigger AI Overviews, the Semrush study shows AI Overviews appeared in only 0.01% of *local* keywords in September 2025. These are compatible findings — broad legal information queries get AI Overviews; specific local service queries ("solicitor Kilkenny") still serve traditional results. This is good news for local SEO: the commercial queries most likely to bring clients remain in traditional search.

#### AEO Content Structure for AI Citation

The answer-first framework — what gets you cited by AI systems:

```markdown
## H2: Question (e.g., "What are the steps for filing for divorce in Ireland?")

**Direct Answer:** [2-3 sentence immediate response]

**Detailed Context:** [Comprehensive explanation]

**Local Application:** [Ireland/county-specific details]

**Professional Attribution:** [Solicitor credentials and experience]
```

**Why this format works:** AI systems are trained to extract concise answers followed by supporting detail. The direct answer at the top gets extracted; the credential attribution signals authority.

#### Authority Signals That AI Systems Weight

> "When articles say 'Written by Sarah Chen, JD, Criminal Defense Attorney, 15 years experience,' they understand you're a legitimate expert."

For Mary Molloy Solicitor, this means the attribution block from the copy skill is not just an SEO signal — it is also an AEO signal. The same block that earns Google E-E-A-T credit makes the content more likely to be cited by ChatGPT and Perplexity.

#### Conversational Language Gets Cited More

> "Conversational language: Content written to answer questions as people ask them gets cited more often. A blog post titled 'What Happens If You're Fired Without Cause?' and written in accessible language outperforms dense legal prose."

This directly reinforces the copy skill principle of plain language over legalese — it is now both a trust signal and an AI citation signal.

#### GEO Technical Requirements

- **Schema Implementation:** LegalService, Attorney/Person, LocalBusiness, FAQPage schemas tell AI systems exactly what the content is about
- **Descriptive URLs:** e.g. `/services/personal-injury/road-traffic-accidents` rather than `/services/pi1`
- **Mobile Optimisation:** Google's AI crawler evaluates mobile usability first — slow or broken mobile experiences are deprioritised for AI citation

### AREA 17: GDPR COMPLIANCE AND SEO

GDPR compliance is directly connected to SEO in two ways: (1) it is a trust signal that affects E-E-A-T, and (2) non-compliance creates risks that could remove the site from search results entirely.

#### Data Protection Requirements on the Website

**Privacy Policy:** Must clearly state what data is collected, how it is used, and on what legal basis. For a solicitor's website, this includes:
- Contact form submissions (what is collected, how long stored, who can access)
- Analytics data (anonymised visitor tracking)
- Cookie tracking

**Cookie Consent:** Proper implementation of a cookie banner — required under GDPR and the Irish ePrivacy Regulations. Analytics cookies (Google Analytics) require explicit consent before firing.

**Contact Forms:** Secure data transmission (HTTPS) and storage. Do not use email address as the only storage for enquiry data.

#### GDPR as an E-E-A-T Signal

A solicitor's website with a clear, well-written privacy policy signals professionalism and trustworthiness — both to Google raters and to prospective clients. A site without one, or with a generic template policy, is a trust negative.

**Specific to Irish solicitors:** The Law Society of Ireland's GDPR guidance for solicitors' practices applies to the website as well as the internal practice. Client data captured through the website (contact forms, enquiry details) falls within the scope of the practice's data protection obligations.

#### Client Communication Security

- Consultation booking forms should use encrypted channels
- Avoid collecting more data than necessary at the enquiry stage (name, contact, brief description of matter is sufficient)
- Email marketing to past clients requires opt-in under GDPR — a newsletter or update service cannot be assumed consent
